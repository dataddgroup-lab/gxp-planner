## The Case AGAINST Building (and Why It's Weak)

### Risk 1: Market Education
**Concern:** Customers don't know they need this yet.  
**Counter:** Customers building facilities TODAY know they have a problem. You're not educating them on "do you need GxP compliance?" (they do). You're educating them on "there's a better way than Excel + black-box consultants" (strong value prop).

### Risk 2: Consultant Resistance
**Concern:** Consultants won't embrace a tool that makes them more transparent.  
**Counter:** The white-label model aligns incentives. Consultants who use your tool scale faster, deliver better results, get paid in commissions on referrals. They'll adopt it if it makes them money.

### Risk 3: Customer Lock-In (Project Ends, Customer Leaves)
**Concern:** After the facility launches, customer has no more use for the system.  
**Counter:** 
- Facilities need ongoing monitoring (compliance updates, audit readiness)
- Companies build multiple facilities (RanchOS effect — once they see value, they use it for the next facility)
- Annual training + compliance updates (recurring SaaS revenue)
- Design the product for recurring revenue, not one-time projects

### Risk 4: Regulatory/Compliance Burden
**Concern:** The system itself must be GxP-compliant (21 CFR 11, EMA Annex 11).  
**Counter:** Built on Supabase (FedRAMP-eligible) + Vercel (FedRAMP). RLS + audit logs + encryption. You're not a pharma manufacturer; you're a software provider. Compliance document exists; customers reviewed existing systems this way.

### Risk 5: Execution
**Concern:** Building, selling, supporting 12+ customers in Year 1 is a lot of work.  
**Counter:** 
- Bootstrap MVP (4 weeks)
- First customer onboarding + support (2 weeks)
- Rinse and repeat
- Hire contractor support after 5 customers
- By Year 2, hire customer success person
- Feasible for 1 founder + 1–2 contractors

**Verdict: RISKS ARE MANAGEABLE ✅**
