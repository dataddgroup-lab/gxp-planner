## EXPANDED PITCH (5 minutes)

### The Problem

GxP facility buildouts are broken:

1. **Chaos** — Validation involves 7 sequential phases (URS → FRS → DS → IQ → OQ → PQ → VSR). Each has dependencies. One missed requirement = months of rework.

2. **No Visibility** — Facility managers see maybe 20% of the work. The rest is done by consultants behind closed doors. You don't know if you're on track until inspection day.

3. **Cost Overruns** — Typical facility project: 6+ months, $500K–$2M, 40+ percent over budget. Why? Hidden scope creep. Risk management is manual (Excel). Change control is reactive. Readiness is guesswork.

4. **Regulatory Risk** — One missed FDA/EMA requirement = facility can't launch. Months of remediation. Millions in lost revenue.

5. **Consultant Dependency** — You hire consultants but have no way to verify they're on the right track. You can't tell if they're padding hours or delivering value.

### Why This Happens

**Existing tools are fragments:**
- Veeva Vault = document management (doesn't guide process)
- SAP/Oracle = ERP (not GxP-specific)
- Excel + manual consulting = black box (no automation, no visibility)

**No single system connects:**
- Facility architecture + systems design
- Validation lifecycle (7 phases + traceability)
- Document control + approvals
- Risk management + mitigation
- Change control + impact assessment
- Training + competency
- Readiness forecasting + drift detection

**Result:** Facility teams are flying blind. Inspections find gaps. Projects slip. Budgets explode.

### The Solution: Universal GxP Facility Planner

We've built an **operating system for facility buildout** that:

1. **Guides the Entire Process** — From facility design through regulatory release. AI assistant knows all regulations (FDA 21 CFR 210/211, EMA Annex 1, ICH Q9, ISO 13485, GAMP 5, data integrity). It anticipates what's needed next. It detects gaps before they become problems.

2. **Gives You Complete Visibility** — Real-time dashboard shows facility progress, validation status, risks, training completion, document approvals, change control, and readiness forecast. No black box. No surprises.

3. **Cuts Timelines in Half** — By automating templating, traceability, and compliance checking, you eliminate rework. Validation timelines drop from 18 months to 9. Startup biotech goes from 12 months to 6.

4. **Reduces Cost & Risk** — Deterministic workflows mean fewer "let me ask my consultant" moments. Risk is managed continuously, not reactively. Changes are assessed automatically. Readiness is forecast mathematically, not guessed.

5. **Keeps Consultants Accountable** — If you work with consultants, they operate inside our platform. You see their work in real-time. You know exactly what's being built, who's doing it, and why. They can't hide.

### How It Works

**For a typical $100K biotech facility buildout:**

- **Month 1:** Facility design + URS creation (AI guides both)
- **Month 2–3:** Validation planning + risk assessment (auto-templated)
- **Month 4–6:** IQ/OQ execution (procedures, test protocols, data collection)
- **Month 7–8:** PQ (performance validation + data analysis)
- **Month 9:** Readiness assessment + inspection prep
- **Month 10:** Release

**Throughout, the platform:**
- Tracks every document, every test, every approval
- Flags risks in real-time
- Forecasts when you'll be inspection-ready
- Alerts you to gaps 60+ days ahead
- Manages all change requests + impact assessments
- Tracks team training + competency

**Team sees:**
- Executive dashboard (readiness %, budget, timeline, blockers)
- QA dashboard (validations, risks, approvals, compliance gaps)
- Operator dashboard (task list, sequencing, dependencies)
- Consultant dashboard (deliverables, hours, billing)

## OBJECTION HANDLING

**Objection 1:** "We already use Veeva Vault. We're not switching."

**Response:** "Veeva is great for document management. It doesn't guide the process. Our platform sits on top of Veeva (or any doc system). It adds the AI consultant layer + readiness forecasting + change impact assessment. Think of it as Veeva + operational intelligence. You keep your document repository. We add the process layer."

---

**Objection 2:** "Our consultants won't like this. They'll lose billable hours."

**Response:** "Consultants actually benefit. They spend less time on boilerplate (templates do that). They spend more time on high-value work—architecture, risk mitigation, problem-solving. Plus, with visibility, clients trust them more and engage them for advisory work beyond the project. Better positioning, better retention, fewer 'am I getting my money's worth' conversations."

---

**Objection 3:** "This sounds complex. How long will it take to implement?"

**Response:** "2–4 weeks. We set up a workspace, you configure your facility parameters (facility type, systems, team), and we seed it with templates + regulations. Your team logs in and starts using it immediately. No 6-month ERP implementation nightmare."

---

**Objection 4:** "What about data security? Regulatory compliance for the platform itself?"

**Response:** "We're built on Supabase (enterprise Postgres), deployed on Vercel (FedRAMP eligible). All data is encrypted at rest and in transit. RLS (row-level security) ensures multi-tenant isolation. Every action is audit-logged (inspection-ready). We comply with 21 CFR 11 requirements for electronic records. Ask for our compliance data sheet."

---

**Objection 5:** "What if we have custom requirements? Your templates won't fit."

**Response:** "Templates are a starting point. You customize them. The AI assistant learns your facility, your company, your risk profile. Over time, it anticipates your specific needs. Plus, we offer professional services—custom workflows, integration with your existing systems, training. You're not locked into templates; you're buying a platform you can shape."
