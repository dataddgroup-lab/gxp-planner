## 1. SITE SELECTION PROCESS

### Step 1: Define Site Requirements

**Procedure:** Identify key criteria — location, infrastructure, zoning, GxP compliance.

**Regulations:** 21 CFR 210/211, 21 CFR 606, ICH Q7A, OSHA 1910.1452/1200/1410

**Roles & Actions:**
- **Project Manager:** Lead process. Define and document site requirements.
- **Legal/Regulatory Specialist:** Review GxP regulatory requirements. Ensure all legal/regulatory criteria included.
- **EHS Specialist:** Review local zoning and environmental regulations. Ensure site meets all EHS requirements.
- **QA Lead:** Ensure all GMP requirements included in selection criteria. Input on QA standards.

**Required Outputs:**
- Site Selection SOP
- Validated site selection criteria
- Pre-defined documentation forms
- Rationale document for criteria

---

### Step 2: Identify Potential Sites

**Procedure:** Compile list of potential sites based on defined criteria.

**Regulations:** 21 CFR 210/211, 21 CFR 606, OSHA 1910.1452/1200/1410

**Roles & Actions:**
- **Project Manager:** Compile site list. Verify all sites meet initial criteria.
- **Real Estate Specialist:** Conduct market research, identify sites, provide initial evaluation.
- **QA Lead:** Review and confirm sites meet GMP standards. Input from QA perspective.

**Required Outputs:**
- Standardized site identification form
- Validated site identification process record
- Documented potential sites with inclusion/exclusion rationale

---

### Step 3: Initial Site Visits / Evaluate Potential Sites

**Procedure:** Conduct preliminary visits to assess basic suitability. Perform detailed evaluation.

**Regulations:** 21 CFR 210/211, 21 CFR 606, OSHA 1910.1452/1200/1410

**Roles & Actions:**
- **Project Manager:** Lead evaluation. Document evaluation criteria.
- **Real Estate Specialist:** Conduct site visits. Gather and provide site-specific data.
- **EHS Specialist:** Perform EHS assessments. Document hazards and concerns.
- **Legal/Regulatory Specialist:** Review zoning/permitting requirements. Address legal/regulatory aspects.
- **QA Lead:** Review infrastructure and GMP compliance. Provide QA input.

**Required Outputs:**
- Site Visit SOP
- Validated site visit process record
- Initial findings document
- Immediate concerns log

---

### Step 4: Detailed Site Analysis

**Procedure:** Environmental assessments, zoning compliance, utility availability.

**Regulations:** 21 CFR 210/211, 21 CFR 606, OSHA 1910.1452/1200/1410

**Roles & Actions:**
- **Project Manager:** Compile shortlist of top sites. Ensure all meet required criteria.
- **Real Estate Specialist:** Confirm shortlisted sites are viable. Site-specific details.
- **EHS Specialist:** Verify EHS compliance. Document potential issues.
- **Legal/Regulatory Specialist:** Confirm legal/regulatory compliance for shortlisted sites.
- **QA Lead:** Verify GMP compliance for shortlisted sites. QA-specific input.

**Required Outputs:**
- Detailed Site Analysis SOP
- Validated site analysis process record
- All environmental, zoning, utility reports documented

---

### Step 5: Cost Analysis

**Procedure:** Detailed cost analysis to evaluate financial feasibility.

**Regulations:** 21 CFR 210/211, 21 CFR 606, OSHA 1910.1452/1200/1410

**Roles & Actions:**
- **Project Manager:** Lead cost analysis process.
- **Real Estate Specialist:** Provide site-specific cost data. Support negotiations.
- **EHS Specialist:** Input on EHS-related costs.
- **Legal/Regulatory Specialist:** Input on regulatory compliance costs.
- **QA Lead:** Input on QA/GMP infrastructure costs.

**Required Outputs:**
- Cost Analysis SOP
- Validated cost analysis process record
- Financial projections and cost-benefit analysis documents

---

### Step 6: Site Selection Decision

**Procedure:** Final decision based on detailed analysis.

**Regulations:** 21 CFR 210/211, 21 CFR 606, OSHA 1910.1452/1200/1410

**Roles & Actions:**
- **Project Manager:** Lead final decision process. Ensure all parties involved.
- **Real Estate Specialist:** Confirm final site. Provide negotiation support.
- **EHS Specialist:** Confirm all EHS requirements met. Site-specific EHS concerns documented.
- **Legal/Regulatory Specialist:** Confirm all legal/regulatory requirements met.
- **QA Lead:** Confirm final site meets GMP standards. Final QA recommendations.

**Required Outputs:**
- Site Selection Decision SOP
- Validated decision-making process record
- Decision rationale document (signed off by all roles)

## APP BUILD IMPLICATIONS

### Database Tables Needed (Phase 1 from this document alone)
- `facilities` — the facility being built
- `facility_phases` — which of the 6 phases the facility is in
- `process_steps` — individual steps within each phase
- `step_roles` — which roles are assigned to each step
- `role_actions` — specific actions per role per step
- `documents` — SOPs, forms, validations, reports (per step)
- `sops` — SOP library (per step, versioned)
- `validations` — validation records (per step/process)
- `permits` — permit tracking (type, issuer, status, date)
- `issues` — issues log from pre-inspection / any step
- `fda_submissions` — FDA submission packages and status
- `regulatory_refs` — 21 CFR, OSHA refs linked to each step

### Screen Flows Needed
1. **Facility Dashboard** — current phase, readiness score, open actions by role
2. **Process Step View** — step detail, assigned roles, required outputs, status
3. **Role Action Checklist** — per user, per step — what they need to do next
4. **Document Manager** — create/upload SOP, form, validation, report per step
5. **Permit Tracker** — all permits, status, expiry, follow-up reminders
6. **FDA Submission Builder** — compile, review, submit, track responses
7. **Issue Log** — flag, assign, resolve, document issues from any step
8. **Regulatory Reference Panel** — always-visible 21 CFR/OSHA refs for current step

### Key Design Principles (from this process)
1. **Every step requires a SOP.** The app generates SOP templates per step.
2. **Every step requires validation.** The app enforces validation sign-off before advancing.
3. **Every step requires documentation.** No step closes without required docs attached.
4. **Every action is role-specific.** Users only see actions relevant to their role.
5. **Every decision is traceable.** Who did what, when, why — immutable audit trail.
6. **Multi-role sign-off is standard.** Most steps require 2-4 roles to complete their actions before the step can close.
