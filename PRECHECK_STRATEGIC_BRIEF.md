## SITUATION

Executive Order 14293 ("Regulatory Relief to Promote Domestic Production of Critical Medicines") launched the FDA PreCheck Pilot Program in 2026.

**Two-phase approach:**
1. **Facility Readiness Phase**: Frequent FDA communication during design/construction/pre-production
2. **Application Submission Phase**: Streamlined CMC review

GxP Planner is positioned to solve PreCheck: Equipment spec → Layout design → Validation → Type V DMF generation → FDA submission.

**But here's the problem:** Building GxP Planner correctly for PreCheck requires FDA regulatory expertise. If built without it:
- Phase 1b–2d could misalign with PreCheck requirements
- Type V DMF generation might be incomplete/inaccurate
- Week 10–12: Discover misalignment, rework required
- June 11 delivery slips to June 25
- Product credibility damaged ("not FDA-aligned")

---

## THE DECISION

**Option A: Engage FDA Expert Week 1**
- Hire FDA regulatory expert (PreCheck/21 CFR knowledge) immediately
- Expert reviews URS/FRS templates, risk framework, equipment database, layout compliance logic
- Expert signs off on Phase 1b, 2a, 2b, 2c, 2d outputs
- Expert produces regulatory guidance document (customer asset)
- Cost: $5K–$15K (52 hours consulting at $100–$300/hr)
- Timeline: June 11 delivery (65–75% confidence)
- Credibility: "FDA-aligned from day 1" (competitive advantage)

**Option B: Build Without FDA Expert**
- No external expert consulting
- Rely on framework (ICH Q9, 21 CFR 211, PreCheck documentation)
- Hope it aligns correctly
- Cost: $0 (saves consulting fees)
- Timeline: June 25 delivery (50–60% confidence, rework risk Week 10–12)
- Credibility: "Built without FDA input" (harder pitch, customer uncertainty)

**Option C: Engage FDA Expert Later (Week 10)**
- Defer expert engagement to Phase 2d review
- Get expert feedback late (after URS/FRS/risk framework locked)
- Rework if misalignment found
- Cost: $5K–$15K (same consulting fees, but rework adds delay)
- Timeline: June 25+ delivery (50% confidence, rework likely)
- Credibility: Damage (customers discover "not FDA-aligned" during Phase 2)
